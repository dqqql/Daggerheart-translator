# THE WISH THIEF

*A tier 1 Daggerheart adventure for 3–5 players, in which the party attends a baby princess's birthday festival and must recover a wondrous gift*

Designed by Rob Hebert

Complexity Rating: ●●○○

# PITCH

Amid the golden glow of a lavish royal birthday festival, unravel a high-society mystery, race through crowded city streets, and confront a deadly foe in a fast-paced adventure of intrigue, magic, and escalating danger.

### OVERVIEW

Today, the city of Bankburgh hosts a once-in-a-generation celebration: the first birthday party festival for Princess Gerdathraeg, heir to the throne! As partygoers fill the streets with frivolity, dignitaries from across the land gather in the royal palace's great hall to present their extravagant gifts in a grand ceremony.

But when a wish-granting tiara powered by the heart of a fallen star is stolen in a moment of magical darkness, joy turns to suspicion. With the castle locked tight and tensions rising, the heroes must uncover the thief hiding among the assembled elites.

The investigation leads to a daring escape, a high-speed chase through the city, and a deadly confrontation with a master thief whose true motive is more mysterious than it first appears.

### TONE & FEEL

Festive, Lighthearted, Opulent, Spectacular

#### TOUCHSTONES

*The Goblin Emperor, Knives Out, The Princess Bride, Reign*

### PLAY STYLE

- Social intrigue and deduction
- High-stakes pursuit
- Shifting, evolving combat

#### ADVENTURE STRUCTURE

This adventure unfolds across six parts:

- 1. **A City in Celebration.** The PCs arrive in Bankburgh with the festival in full swing and must hurry to the city center to reach the palace before the princess's gifting ceremony begins.
- 2. **The Gifting Ceremony.** One by one, the assembled dignitaries present their gifts to the princess. When her royal parents present their gift, the room is plunged into darkness—and the gift is stolen!
- 3. **Lockdown.** The hall is sealed, and the royal advisor recruits the party to investigate and identify the thief.
- 4. **Rundown.** The thief escapes, pursued by the party through the streets of Bankburgh as the chaos of the festival transforms the city into a gauntlet.
- 5. **Showdown.** Cornered at the docks, the thief and her cohorts play keep-away with the tiara as they face off against the party.
- 6. **Return & Reward.** The party returns the tiara to the king and queen, who offer them royal boons in gratitude.

# CHARACTERS & LOCATIONS

*The following NPCs and key locations play important roles in this adventure.*

### CROWN PRINCESS "GERDI" GERDATHRAEG

*Highborne Goblin (she/her) The Birthday Girl*

A small child seated at the center of overwhelming grandeur, Gerdi watches the world with bright, curious eyes. She laughs freely and reaches for anything that glitters. Though she doesn't speak in full sentences yet, her expressions and gestures communicate clearly. At moments, Gerdi seems unusually still—especially when magic is nearby—as though she senses something others cannot.

*Which of the PCs does she take a shine to?*

### KING TERLOC

*Seaborne Goblin (he/him) Gerdi's Dad*

Broad-shouldered and imposing (for a goblin), King Terloc carries himself with the weight of history. His armor is ceremonial, etched with runes and stories of past victories. His voice is stern and authoritative, but never cruel. He speaks like one accustomed to being obeyed, but who values competence over flattery.

*King Terloc wasn't born to the crown of Bankburgh; how did he acquire it?*

#### QUEEN LIRICA

*Ridgeborne Goblin (she/her) Gerdi's Mom*

Graceful and radiant, Queen Lirica seems almost otherworldly. Her presence softens the severity of the throne, but her gaze is as icy and unyielding as her homeland. She speaks warmly and gently, often noticing what others overlook.

*Queen Lirica publicly champions the common folk; which of her policies has drawn the most ire from the nobles?*

#### PARSON REDSTRING

*Orderborne Firbolg (he/him) Royal Advisor to the King and Queen of Bankburgh*

A tall, austere figure draped in layered robes of deep purple and gold, Redstring slides silently through the court like a shadow cast by the throne itself. His eyes are sharp, his posture is immaculate, and his presence quietly commands attention without demanding it.

*How does Parson Redstring know—or know of—the party?*

### STABITHA DARKFEATHER

*Slyborne Faerie (she/her) Illusionist, Assassin, and Thief*

Stabitha glamours her appearance to mimic others and initially appears in the guise of a noble to steal the tiara. She's a calculating operative driven by purpose over passion. Once discovered, she becomes quick and efficient, driven only toward escape. When cornered, she fights. When defeated, she reveals her true form: a tall faerie with hornet-like features.

*What tell does Stabitha always have, regardless of how she's glamoured herself?*

### BANKBURGH, CROSSROADS OF THE WORLD

Bankburgh thrives where opportunity and ambition meet.

Built at the crossroads of major trade routes and known for its ingenious tiered canals and vaulted stone bridges, the city has grown rich from the steady migration and accumulation of goods, coin, and ideas.

River barges glide beneath streets alive with the clamor of commerce, while elevated walkways crisscross whole districts stacked atop one another. Everywhere, something is being bought, sold, or celebrated.

But Bankburgh's true strength is its openness. Artisans, nobles, performers, and opportunists from across the world pass through its gates, each leaving something behind: an ancient tradition, a new discovery, or a valuable secret. The result is a city that feels larger than life, vibrant and unpredictable, where fortunes can be made overnight… or vanish just as quickly.

At the city's heart, the royal castle gleams above the bustle, a testament to the prosperity Bankburgh enjoys.

# THE GIFTING CEREMONY

ACT TWO

*In this act, the players create the gifts Princess Gerdi receives and define the nobles who present them.*

## THE GREAT HALL

The gifting ceremony takes place in the great hall of the royal castle. This is a large rectangular space with its main doors at one end and the dais on which the thrones sit at the other. Running along either side are spacious galleries separated from the wide central aisle by marble colonnade that are marked along the surface with iridescent runes.

**The colonnade forms a magical barrier that blocks spell effects from crossing over.** This barrier doesn't prevent people from casting spells inside the great hall, but it does prevent effects from crossing over from the central aisle to the galleries on either side—or vice versa. Think of this barrier like soundproof glass, which doesn't prevent anyone from talking, it just prevents everyone on the other side of it from hearing them.

This barrier was chosen instead of an antimagic field because it offers the royals protection from would-be magical assassins without negating desired magical effects. This barrier is key to limiting the number of suspects the party has to deal with in the upcoming investigation.

While the barrier's operation is subtle, any PC with a Spellcast trait who inspects the column runes can figure out the basics of how it works.

### WHAT'S ABOUT TO HAPPEN

In the next few moments, Parson Redstring tries to place the tiara onto Gerdi's head—but the Wish Thief, Stabitha Darkfeather, conjures a magical darkness that momentarily blocks out all light and sound, then uses the opportunity to steal the tiara.

She's been glamoured as one of the NPCs throughout the party, and she had intended to simply walk out with the tiara hidden on her person as the great hall erupted in chaos. ```

# LOCKDOWN

ACT THREE

*In this act, the great hall is sealed so the thief can be found, and the party undertakes an investigation.*

*Start the scene by reading the following aloud:*

```
The king and queen leap into action, commanding their sentries to seal the great hall. Commoners clam up, while nobles protest. The guards move swiftly through the chamber, checking servants, entertainers, and attendants with practiced efficiency. One by one, they're cleared frightened and confused, but innocent.

Parson Redstring watches in silence, then speaks: "This crime required precise timing and positioning." His gaze shifts to the gathered nobles. "There is a magical barrier surrounding this section of the great hall. The source of that magical darkness must be someone at the center of the ceremony."

A hush falls over the room. No one says it aloud, but the meaning is clear: The thief is still here. And they are one of the honored guests.

Parson Redstring approaches your party and whispers to you conspiratorially. "One of these nobles has taken the tiara. You brought the tiara here in the first place, so you are the only ones above suspicion. I need you to find the culprit—without triggering a political crisis."

You subtly scan the nobles closest to the dais. Each one seems suspicious in their own way.
```

Ask the players: Who do you want to interrogate first?

> *Tip: Be forthright with the players: "The thief is clearly one of the nobles whom you saw present a gift to Gerdi." Do the following from this point on:*
> - Give clues that point to nobles only.
> - Use other NPCs to cast or confirm suspicions, not to confuse the players.
> - If players fixate on servants, resolve quickly and redirect.

## THE INVESTIGATION

Activate an Investigation Countdown with a starting value equal to 1 + the number of players (for example, if there are four players, the countdown should start at 5). Tick it down after each failed action roll. When the countdown triggers, the Wish Thief makes their move (see "Reveal & Escape" on page 11).

### THE USUAL SUSPECTS

Use the nobles introduced during the gifting ceremony as your primary suspects. Any one of them can become the culprit if the fiction supports it, so let the players gravitate toward whomever they find interesting or suspicious.

### THE QUANTUM CULPRIT

*Don't preselect the thief*. Instead, let the culprit be the first suspect the players commit to on the basis of real evidence. Try to do the following during the investigation:

- Seed suspicion broadly
- Reinforce player theories
- Quietly align clues to support their conclusion

Don't introduce misleading clues or red herrings; the players will provide plenty of their own.

If the party strongly suspects someone other than the nobles, such as Parson Redstring himself, use the blank NPC cards to add them to the lineup!

### THE GAMEPLAY LOOP

Run the investigation as a series of looped steps:

- 1. **Determine the Player's Approach.** Ask the players what they want to do. For instance, they might question a noble, scan the room, collect and examine physical evidence, or use magic.
- 2. **Establish the Stakes.** Ask follow-up questions to clarify what the PC is trying to learn and what's at risk if they fail.
- 3. **Resolve the Attempt.** Have the player make an action roll if necessary. Action rolls in this scene have a default Difficulty of 14.
- 4. **Deliver Information.** On a success, give the player a clear answer or a solid lead—for example, a trustworthy source contradicts a noble's testimony, magical traces have been left near Gerdi, or a suspect refuses to cooperate with the investigation. On a failure, give the player partial or ambiguous information, then tick down the Investigation Countdown.
- 5. **Repeat.** Return to Step 1.

As the interrogation continues, escalate the tension by having the nobles grow impatient and eventually demand to be released. Suspects might start accusing one another, potentially rising to violence. Redstring or the royal family's faith in the party might falter. Use Redstring to refocus stalled players, ask pointed questions, or reinforce the urgency of the situation. For example, Redstring might say:

- *"I think you're on the right track…"*
- *"What do these inconsistencies suggest?"*
- *"Quickly, my friends! Time is not our ally!"*
- *"Is there something we've overlooked?"*

Don't solve the mystery for the players, just use Redstring to subtly shift their attention.

## BANKBURGH CHASE

### *Tier 1 Event*

*Pursuing the Wish Thief through Bankburgh during Gerdi's birthday festival*

**Impulses:** Disorient and distract, put distance between pursuer and prey, turn the party around

**Difficulty:** 12

**Potential Adversaries:** City Watch (Archer Guard, Bladed Guard), Petty Crooks (Jagged Knife Bandit, Jagged Knife Lackey), Revelers (Courtesan, Courtier, Masked Thief, Merchant Baron, Petty Noble)

#### FEATURES

*The Game Is Afoot – Passive.* At the start of the scene, activate two dynamic countdowns: a Progress Countdown (8) and a Consequence Countdown (5). If the PCs identified the thief before the previous scene's Investigation Countdown triggered, tick down the Progress Countdown by 2. See "Ending the Chase" for what happens when the countdowns trigger.

*Does the thief rely more on stealth, speed, or acrobatics to escape? What part of town does she seem to be headed for?*

*Crowd Surge – Action.* **Spend a Fear** to have the tide of festivalgoers split a PC off from the party.

*Where are the revelers heading? How does the lost PC try to regain their bearings and reconnect with the party?*

*Hide in Plain Sight – Action.* **Spend a Fear** to have the thief disguise herself as a random bystander and fade into the sea of faces. The closest PC must succeed on an Instinct Reaction Roll to spot her or the Consequence Countdown ticks down by 1.

*Who does the thief disguise herself as? In her haste, how does she give herself away?*

### ENDING THE CHASE

End the chase when one or both of the countdowns trigger:

- **If the Consequence Countdown triggers first,** the thief arrives at the dockyard and sets a trap for the party; gain 2 Fear and immediately shift the spotlight to one of the adversaries as they reveal themselves.
- **If the Progress Countdown triggers first,** the party cuts the thief off at the pass and is waiting for her when she arrives at the dockyard. The players choose a PC to gain the spotlight, a Hope, and advantage on their first action roll.
- **If both countdowns trigger simultaneously**, you gain a Fear, and the spotlight swings to the players.

# RETURN & REWARD

ACT SIX

*When the PCs return with the tiara (and possibly Stabitha) to the great hall of the Bankburgh royal palace, King Terloc, Queen Lirica, and Parson Redstring greet their arrival.*

*When they enter the palace, read the following aloud:*

```
As you enter the great hall, the gathered nobles part before you, their whispers trailing in your wake. At the far end, King Terloc and Queen Lirica rise as you approach. For a moment, no one speaks. Then you present the tiara, and the entire assemblage breathes a collective sigh of relief.

Terloc steps forward, taking the tiara in both hands. His grip tightens, just slightly, before he exhales *(GM Tip: don't forget to do the voices!)*: "You have done this kingdom a great service."

Beside him, Lirica's gaze softens as she looks to you measured, but undeniably grateful. "You have returned more than a gift," she says quietly. "You have preserved our future." She kneels, gently placing the tiara upon Princess Gerdi's head.

The gemstone catches the light—and for just a moment, the colors within shift ever so slightly. Gerdi giggles, reaching up with both hands, delighted. The tension in the hall dissolves into relieved laughter and applause.

Lirica inclines her head. "You will always be welcome in Bankburgh."

The court regards you now with respect—and perhaps expectation?

Terloc turns back to you. "Name your reward," he says. "Gold, land, title… or a favor yet to be called."
```

Let the PCs discuss amongst themselves what boon they'd like from the king and queen. They might ask for one of the following:

- Property in Bankburgh (which could serve as a base of operations for future endeavors)
- A role within the court to further engage with the politics of the city
- Loot (perhaps in the form of one of the gifts presented to Gerdi earlier)
- Gold, and lots of it!

The king and queen are truly thankful to the party, and grant them their boon before raising a toast to them. Try to end the scene on a high point, such as a laugh line or callback. If all else fails, describe Gerdi doing something cute, like honking a PC's nose or gurgling out her first full sentence.
